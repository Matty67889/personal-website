Multiple Choice Definition Game

About

This game features a game where you select definitions of words to test your knowledge. It was designed to help users learn more words by testing their ability to remember the definitions of the words.

How to Play

In the box below, you will see a word in bold at the top, followed by 4 answer choices. To play, select the correct answer choice and then click the Check Answer button to check if your choice was correct. To generate a new word, click on the New Word button.

The game usually draws from a pool of standard English words, but if you wish to change the pool of words that the game generates words from to word from the Dictionary of Obscure Sorrows, click the on/off switch. When the switch is toggled to on, words from the Dictionary of Obscure Sorrows will be used. See the Dictionary of Obscure Sorrows Option section for more information on the effects of the switch.

Dictionary of Obscure Sorrows Option

The Dictionary of Obscure Sorrows is a collection of words developed by John Koenig to explain some of the deeper emotions that humans experience that cannot quite be explained with standard dictionary words. If you would like a copy of the book, it is available on Amazon, Barnes & Noble, and quite possibly your local bookstore. You can also go to Simon & Schuster to get a copy of the book.

I DO NOT claim rights to the Dictionary of Obscure Sorrows, or any of the words used in it. I am simply using the words in the dictionary for the game so that others can memorize them and spread the magic of expressed emotion in their daily lives. All credits for this work of art goes to John Koenig.
