About Me

I am a Freshman at Cornell University studying Computer Science. I hope to use my the knowledge that I gain from coding technology to develop ways to make education easier and more accessible for people around the world. Apart from Computer Science, I am interested in learning concepts in Psychology. It is an interesting field to me as it helps me learn about how are brains work, and I also think that it will allow aid me in my goal of making learning easier for people. In my free time, I read, write, and play video games.

My interest in computer science budded when I was young and I became enamored by how cool it was to see coding in movies. When I first entered high school, my interest was nurtured when I got my first taste of coding. I realized that although it was difficult at times, it was still rewarding and fun to engage in. In college, I am hoping to make my interest blossom by studying it as my major.

When I read books, I become enamored by the way authors string together words into a glorious symphony of text. They suck me into their fantasy world they've created and amaze me with unexpected twists and adorable love stories. It amazes me that authors manage to create suspense and trepidation like that of a movie even in text where everything that happens is laid out for the reader to see. Their writing prowess has inspired me to try to achieve something as good on paper. I tried to write a book when I was younger, and still write down my story ideas to this day.

Throughout our lives we're told that everyone is unique, and that concept has always intrigued me. We all think in different ways, react differently to stimuli, are interested in different activities, and make different decisions based on our past experiences. I want to learn more about what goes on in our brain, and how our environment causes us to explore different paths in our lives. With this knowledge, I will not only achieve a greater understanding of our world, but I may also be able to use it to find the most effective ways to educate people with technology, which is something I hope to do.

Games have always been a big part of my life, whether they be digital, table based, or satirical. They challenge my mind by presenting me with unfamiliar situations and force me to think critically. Oftentimes I am able to apply the thinking I use in games to make things easier to understand in real life. When I do play games, they usually involve strategy. One of my favorites is Dragon Quest 9, an RPG about a hero's quest to rid the world of evil. It's outlandish humor and extensive vocabulary has taught me a lot, and contributed to making me who I am today.
